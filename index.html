Barbu: Rules and Instructions

Barbu is a classic trick‐taking card game played with a standard deck of 52 cards. The game is known for its dynamic gameplay, as players navigate through different contracts with varying objectives and scoring rules. The goal is to end the game with the lowest total score.

Players and Setup

  • Number of Players: 3 to 6 (4 is optimal)
  • Each player will be the dealer in rotation.
  • Every player must play each contract exactly once.
  • The number of rounds is equal to the number of contracts × number of players.
  • Players can choose which contracts to include, but all must select the same set of contracts.

Card Distribution

  • 3 Players: 48 cards (16 each; remove all 2s)
  • 4 Players: 52 cards (standard deck)
  • 5 Players: 50 cards (remove 2♣ and 2♦)
  • 6 Players: 48 cards (remove all 2s)

Contracts (Mini-Games)

Barbu is played over 7 distinct contracts, each with its own rules and scoring system:

  1. King of Spades
  2. Hearts
  3. Queens
  4. Tricks
  5. Last Two Tricks
  6. Domino
  7. Barbu

Each contract is played separately, except for Barbu, which combines multiple rules.

Strategy Tips

  • Plan Ahead: Keep track of remaining contracts.
  • Be Flexible: Adapt strategy to the current contract.
  • Avoid Risk: Especially in Barbu rounds.
  • Watch Opponents: Anticipate traps in King of Spades and Queens.
